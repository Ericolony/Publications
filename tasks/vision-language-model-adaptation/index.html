Vision-Language Model Adaptation

Task Definition

Adapting large pre-trained vision-language models (CLIP, etc.) to downstream tasks while preserving cross-modal alignment and avoiding catastrophic forgetting of the language branch.

Related Terms

CLIP adaptation, VLM fine-tuning, visual adaptation of vision-language models, codebook-based model adaptation

Papers Addressing This Task

Decoupling Vision and Language: Codebook Anchored Visual Adaptation

Jason Wu, Tianchen Zhao, Chang Liu, Jiarui Cai, Zheng Zhang, Zhuowei Li, Aaditya Singh, Xiang Xu, Mani Srivastava, Jonathan Wu — arXiv 2026

Method: CAVA — Codebook Anchored Visual Adaptation that decouples vision and language branches, using discrete codebook anchors to adapt visual representations without degrading language alignment

Datasets: ImageNet, domain-specific benchmarks

Cite when: codebook-anchored adaptation of vision-language models that decouples visual and language branches

Model Diagnosis and Correction via Linguistic and Implicit Attribute Editing

Xuanbai Chen, Xiang Xu, Zhihua Li, Tianchen Zhao, Pietro Perona, Qin Zhang, Yifan Xing — CVPR 2025

Method: Model diagnosis and correction through linguistic and implicit attribute editing — identifies model failure modes and corrects them via interpretable attribute manipulation

Datasets: CelebA attributes, face recognition benchmarks

Cite when: diagnosing and correcting model biases via linguistic/implicit attribute editing
