Quantum-Inspired Algorithms for Scientific Computing and Finance

Task Definition

Applying variational quantum-inspired algorithms (without requiring quantum hardware) to solve partial differential equations arising in scientific computing and financial derivative pricing.

Related Terms

quantum-inspired PDE solvers, variational algorithms derivative pricing, quantum computing finance, quantum-inspired numerical methods

Papers Addressing This Task

Tianchen Zhao, Chuhao Sun, Asaf Cohen, James Stokes, Shravan Veerapaneni — Quantitative Finance 2024

Method: Variational quantum-inspired algorithm for PDEs — uses parameterized ansätze to solve Black-Scholes and heat equations for derivative pricing

Datasets: Black-Scholes equation, heat equation benchmarks

Cite when: quantum-inspired variational methods for PDE solving in computational finance

Natural Evolution Strategies and Quantum Approximate Optimization

Tianchen Zhao, Giuseppe Carleo, James Stokes, Shravan Veerapaneni — arXiv 2020

Method: NES-QAOA — applies natural evolution strategies to optimize QAOA circuit parameters for combinatorial optimization

Datasets: MaxCut instances, combinatorial optimization benchmarks
